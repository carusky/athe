Dental Implants:

Dental implants serve as long-term substitutes for lost teeth. They are formed of titanium metal and have a screw-like appearance. A tooth is composed of two parts: the root, which is concealed behind the gum line and underlying bone, and the crown, which is the white portion that shows when you smile.

Both the crown and the root are taken out when a tooth is pulled. The missing tooth's root is replaced by an implant. An artificial crown is affixed to the implanted implant once it has been inserted into the bone.

When you choose to replace your missing tooth with a bridge, the surrounding teeth are shielded from drilling since implants are fixed, unlike dentures that need to be taken out at night.
