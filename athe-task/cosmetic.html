Teeth Whitening:

Teeth whitening is a popular cosmetic dentistry treatment that uses bleaching agents to lighten teeth's original shade without damaging tooth tissue. It can be used to address stains from colored foods, drinks, medication, smoking, or aging. However, in cases of tooth-staining disturbances or trauma, professional-grade materials are the safest option. There are two main types of whitening processes.

There are 2 main types of whitening processes

In Office

Tooth whitening involves an initial assessment, hardened rubber gel on gums, and a special light-accelerated procedure. It takes an hour, broken into 15-minute sessions with new gel applied each time.

Take-Home Whitening

At-home whitening is cost-effective and comparable to in-office whitening, but may take a few weeks due to lower concentration of bleaching agents.

Teeth Straightening:

Teeth straightening involves using metal braces to move or reposition teeth, with a wire passing through the bracket and adjusted regularly to achieve the desired position.

Invisible braces, also known as clear aligners, are transparent trays that help straighten teeth, are less visible than traditional metal braces, and are removable.

Metal braces are generally more effective than aligners in complex cases.

Veneers:

A veneer is a thin material bonded to a natural tooth, improving its appearance. It's beneficial for chipped, shorter, or discolored teeth, as well as closing gaps and correcting poorly positioned teeth.

Veneers can either be made from porcelain or composite resin.

Porcelain

Advancements in porcelain technology enable the creation of thin, natural-looking, and durable veneers that require minimal tooth shaven down for placement.

Composite Resin

Composite veneers offer an affordable, natural-looking alternative to porcelain veneers, ideal for chipped teeth as they don't require extensive shaves, making them a conservative option.

A veneer placement involves two visits: tooth preparation, color determination, mouth copy, and bonding after satisfaction. The second appointment is when the veneer is ready and bonded.
