Teeth Cleaning:

Hardened deposits that accumulate on and around your teeth over time are removed by professional cleaning. Bacteria in these deposits gradually erode the supporting tissues of your teeth. If nothing is done for a lengthy period, this might lead to wobbly teeth and bleeding during brushing.

Although many people take pride in never having seen the dentist, your toothbrush will no longer function in certain places once these deposits harden. Kindly stop by for a check-up and cleaning; prompt maintenance helps avoid regrets down the road.

Dental Filling:

Tooth decay is a bacterial infection of the tooth. These bacteria produce acids that erode the tooth's structure and leave holes in it. The substance used to repair these holes, trauma-related chipping, or tooth wear is called a filling.

The color of this filler substance could be gold, silver, or white. Because white fillings look more natural and complement teeth, they are the most commonly utilized type these days.

When it's decided that you require a filling, you'll be informed of your options and helped to make the best decision.

A crown, sometimes referred to as a cap, is a covering that is applied to a tooth that has been broken, weakened, or worn down in order to give it strength and return it to its natural form. For the crown to fit, the tooth that needs it will need to be completely cut down.

A crown requires two visits minimum. The tooth is made smaller on the first appointment, and a copy of the crown preparation is taken. After you have viewed it and are happy with it, your crown will be bonded at your second appointment.
